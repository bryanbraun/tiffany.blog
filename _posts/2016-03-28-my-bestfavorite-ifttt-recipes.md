<em>Caveat: IFTTT has been in a battle with a few of its services for using the developers it relies on and making them create custom APIs that IFTTT claims all rights to.</em>

<em>As an aspiring developer, this angers me. But there is no real alternative. Part of my workflow was Pinboard but IFTTT is sunsetting the Pinboard service because the Pinboard dev has integrity and wishes to retain the rights to his intellectual property.</em>

<em>With this said, I still use it as it is key to my workflow.</em>

The above caveat aside, IFTTT remains a significant tool in home and web service automation. It is the glue to the Internet of Things and remains an important part of many workflows, including my own.

I was asked on Twitter to list some of my favorite recipes. So I am listing them below with a little description.

<a id="embed_recipe-402213" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_34" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/402213-know-when-issues-come-up-on-github" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/402213" alt="IFTTT Recipe: Know When Issues Come Up on GitHub connects github to google-calendar" width="370px" /></a>

This is so I can keep up with issues on my GitHub repos and when the issues first were reported.

<a id="embed_recipe-402212" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_32" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/402212-feedly-read-laters-to-instapaper" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/402212" alt="IFTTT Recipe: Feedly Read Laters to Instapaper connects feedly to instapaper" width="370px" /></a><script src="//ifttt.com/assets/embed_recipe.js" async="" type="text/javascript"></script>

This is for my reading workflow, inspired in part by Mike Vardy on the Productivityist Podcast. Interesting articles I'd like to write about I star in Reeder, which is hooked up to Feedly and automatically added to an Instapaper folder called <em>To write</em>. I would have archived those articles for safe keeping in Pinboard but....

<a id="embed_recipe-402210" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_28" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/402210-cards-to-gdrive-for-archival" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/402210" alt="IFTTT Recipe: Cards to GDrive for Archival connects trello to google-drive" width="370px" /></a><script src="//ifttt.com/assets/embed_recipe.js" async="" type="text/javascript"></script>

This is a way for me to archive ideas and cards from an important Trello Board for various projects. It will append every card to one document instead of creating new ones with each card created.

<a id="embed_recipe-402209" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_49" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/402209-keep-track-of-your-goodreads-activity-in-day-one" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/402209" alt="IFTTT Recipe: Keep Track of Your Goodreads activity in Day One. connects feed to day-one" width="370px" /></a><script src="//ifttt.com/assets/embed_recipe.js" async="" type="text/javascript"></script>

I <em>love</em> Goodreads. I am a voracious reader of books and I need some way to share and keep track of what I am reading. I have <em>calibre</em> for ebooks and <em>Book Collector</em> for paperbacks, hardbacks, and ebooks but I like the Goodreads challenges and the ability to review books, which I can then sync to my calibre library. This is a way to keep track of my Goodreads habits and updates.

<a id="embed_recipe-402208" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_32" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/402208-push-highlights-to-your-computer" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/402208" alt="IFTTT Recipe: Push Highlights to Your Computer connects instapaper to pushbullet" width="370px" /></a><script src="//ifttt.com/assets/embed_recipe.js" async="" type="text/javascript"></script>

This is a way for me to push highlights of important talking points from Instapaper to my computer to add to Notes.app and then write about those points. Pushbullet is an excellent app for this. I am starting to use DeskConnect for more file exchanges but DeskConnect does not have IFTTT integration, nor should they.

<a id="embed_recipe-402207" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_18" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/402207-wp-post-to-day-one" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/402207" alt="IFTTT Recipe: WP Post to Day One connects wordpress to day-one" width="370px" /></a><script src="//ifttt.com/assets/embed_recipe.js" async="" type="text/javascript"></script>

I use this recipe to keep a log of my Code Newbie in Pittsburgh posts in Day One. Day One is my journaling app of choice. They just opened up to the IFTTT platform which I had been waiting a long time for. I have a myriad of recipes for Day One but this is one of the more important ones.

<a id="embed_recipe-402206" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_39" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/402206-end-your-focustime-session-with-todoist" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/402206" alt="IFTTT Recipe: End Your FocusTime Session with Todoist connects todoist to rescuetime" width="370px" /></a><script src="//ifttt.com/assets/embed_recipe.js" async="" type="text/javascript"></script>

I use RescueTime menubar app to keep track of my productivity and I pay for the Premium account, which to me, is worth it. I can start a FocusTime session, which blocks my access to whatever site I deem very distracting in RescueTime for whatever time I want with an IFTTT recipe that turns it on when I search my calendar for a specific event. When I complete the event, I check it off in Todoist and it stops the FocusTime Session, even if time isn't up.

<a id="embed_recipe-402205" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_38" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/402205-gcal-search-event-to-focustime-session" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/402205" alt="IFTTT Recipe: GCal Search Event to FocusTime Session connects google-calendar to rescuetime" width="370px" /></a><script src="//ifttt.com/assets/embed_recipe.js" async="" type="text/javascript"></script>

This is the recipe I mentioned above.

<a id="embed_recipe-309289" class="embed_recipe embed_recipe-l_26" href="https://ifttt.com/view_embed_recipe/309289-add-a-wp-post-to-pinterest" target="_blank"><img style="max-width: 100%;" src="https://ifttt.com/recipe_embed_img/309289" alt="IFTTT Recipe: Add a WP Post to Pinterest connects wordpress to pinterest" width="370px" /></a><script src="//ifttt.com/assets/embed_recipe.js" async="" type="text/javascript"></script>

And lastly, this is a recipe I use to post my Code Newbie in Pittsburgh posts to Pinterest. They say Pinterest is a good marketing tool and I did have some success on one of my posts there. Need to have good enough graphics to entice and my blog is so geeky I am sure it doesn't appeal to the moms on Pinterest.

So these are my recipes. What are your favorites?
